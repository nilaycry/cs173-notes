Once truth values are treated as abstract objects, the next question is how they behave under logical operations.

Truth tables are the first systematic answer.

## What a Truth Table Records

A truth table records the output of an expression for every possible assignment of truth values to its input sentences.

If an expression depends on one sentence, the table lists the two possible input values.

If it depends on two sentences, the table lists the four possible input pairs.

The point is not to make the notation look mechanical. The point is to record, in one place, every possible semantic case.

## Why the Course Uses Them Early

At the start of the unit, truth tables do two jobs well.

First, they make the meaning of the logical connectives explicit.

Second, they give a direct way to test whether two expressions always behave the same way, or whether an expression is always true.

That makes them a strong early tool for learning the semantics of logic.

## A Simple Example

For negation, the table is:

$$
\begin{array}{c|c}
p & \neg p \\
\hline
\top & \bot \\
\bot & \top
\end{array}
$$

This table says exactly what negation does. It reverses the truth value.

## What a Full Table Is Doing

A larger table follows the same idea. If an expression uses two sentences, then the table must account for all four possible assignments:

$$
(\top,\top), \quad (\top,\bot), \quad (\bot,\top), \quad (\bot,\bot)
$$

Each row is one possible semantic case. The final column records how the whole expression behaves in that case.

## Why Truth Tables Matter Less Later

Truth tables are important here, but they will not stay central for the whole course.

Later parts of CS 173 rely much more on definitions, proof structure, arbitrary objects, and statement form than on full truth-table computations.

So this note should be read as an early-unit tool, not as the main method for the course as a whole.

## Transition

Now that the table method is in place, the logical connectives can be introduced one by one as operations on truth values.
